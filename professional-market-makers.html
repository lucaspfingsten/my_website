Professional Market Makers

Instead of utilizing an automated market maker, these approaches introduce a professional market maker to handle trades and are expected to execute them at market price. Generally, these approaches involve off-chain agreements on exchange rates and on-chain transaction execution.

Positives

  • Almost no increase in transaction cost.
  • No jostling

Negatives

  • Scope limited to transaction reordering manipulations
  • Decrease in goodput
  • Delay increases
  • Decentralization decreases
  • Security in case of byzantine market makers

Methods

MEV-Share

Operated by Flashbots, this service enables the redistribution of MEV to users, transaction originators, or other designated parties. Users submit their transactions to a Mmatchmaker, who partially discloses transaction details to searchers. Searcherâ€™s craft MEV-generating transactions and send them back to the matchmaker, who completes the bundle with the users transactions and sets conditions for MEV redistribution. The completed bundle is then sent to block builders, who include it in a block and enforce the redistribution conditions, thus rewarding users with a share of the MEV generated by their transactions.

FairMM

FairMM is designed to prevent frontrunning through off-chain communication and on-chain settlement. Traders send orders to the market maker off-chain. These orders are hashed and committed on-chain. After a predefined period, the actual orders are revealed and executed based on the prior commitments.

Hashflow

Hashflow is a DEX where users can trade assets across different blockchains with guaranteed price quotes. Unlike typical AMMs used in many DEXs, Hashflow leverages a request-for-quote model to provide traders with guaranteed price quotes directly from professional market makers.

Rook

Rook is a DeFi protocol aggregates liquidity and orders into a single pool where they are matched. Trades execute in a manner that conceals their intent until the transaction is finalized, to prevent manipulation by other actors. Rook incentivizes participants, to collaborate in capturing MEV opportunities and distributes the profits among them and ROOK token holders.

FM-AMM

The proposed protocol in the paper introduces a Function-Maximizing Automated Market Maker (FM-AMM) design that aggregates trades in batches. This approach sets a new marginal price for each batch, determined by maximizing a predefined function, aiming to establish fair equilibrium prices. The FM-AMM design addresses common issues in current AMM systems like arbitrage and sandwich attacks by eliminating immediate price impact. It also demonstrates better outcomes in liquidity provision compared to traditional AMMs, considering various trading scenarios and market conditions. This innovative design could revolutionize automated market making by improving efficiency and fairness in trade executions. (TBC)
