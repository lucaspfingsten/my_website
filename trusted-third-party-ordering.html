Trusted Third Party Ordering

Trusted third party ordering refers to schemes that entrust a trusted third party with the ordering. Transactions are sent directly to the trusted third party who then orders them. Thus, these schemes order transactions efficiently while compromising decentralization and security.

Positives

  • No delay
  • Almost no increased cost
  • Goodput stays unchanged
  • Jostling very low
  • Good Scope

Negatives

  • Decentralization impacted
  • Security in case of byzantine third party

Methods

MEV Protect

Multiple providers (Flashbots, Eden, bloXroute, Open MEV, etc.) offer MEV protection services. Users send their transactions directly to the service provider. Thus, the transaction is no longer sent to the public mempool where it can be front run by bots. Instead, these services send bundles directly to miners/validators who have entered into agreements with the respective provider. Some DEXes integrate these services into their UI to make them easily accessible for users.

CowSwap

CowSwap is a decentralized exchange aggregator that tackles MEV by facilitating trading without the traditional order book or AMM. Instead of executing orders immediately, CowSwap matches opposite orders from users within a batch auction system, allowing for direct swaps when a coincidence of wants is found, reducing slippage and eliminating the opportunity for miners to prioritize transactions for profit. If direct matches aren't found, CowSwap uses its underlying Gnosis Protocol to find the best price across several DEXs, ensuring users still get favourable trade conditions while minimizing the impact on the market price and saving on transaction fees.

Gnosis

Gnosis Protocol is a decentralized exchange protocol designed to enable batch auctions on the Ethereum blockchain. It employs a mechanism that collects multiple buy and sell orders over a fixed period into a single batch and then uses a price-finding algorithm to determine the optimal trade prices and match orders.
