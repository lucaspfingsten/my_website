Optimized Trade Execution

Optimized trade execution schemes are the least invasive approach to prevent front-running. These approaches adjust the trade parameters to make potential front-running attacks unprofitable. Thus, these schemes have negligible impact on the underlying blockchain system but are limited in scope.

Positives

  • No impact on decentralization
  • No increase in transaction cost
  • Unchanged blockchain's goodput
  • Unchanged blockchain's transaction ordering

Negatives

  • Scope limited to specific applications
  • Slight transaction delay increase
  • Increased competition among similar transactions

Methods

A2MM

A2MM is an advanced automated market maker system that aims to minimize arbitrage opportunities between itself and other AMM exchanges. When a swap occurs it checks the size of the swap to determine if synchronization with among multiple AMMs is necessary, after which it executes optimal routing. In scenarios where a trader's balance isn't sufficient for arbitrage, a flash loan can be invoked. A2MM, therefore, not only facilitates regular trading of assets between liquidity providers and takers but also efficiently handles arbitrage and routing across multiple AMMs, aiming to maximize trading benefits for its users.

Order Splits as Mitigation Strategy

This protocol outlines the implementation of order splitting as a defense mechanism against sandwich attacks in decentralized exchanges. The strategy involves breaking down large orders into smaller segments to minimize the profitability of sandwich attacks by reducing the price difference caused by the transaction and exchange fees. The process of determining optimal input amounts for split orders takes into account the significance of disregarding transaction fees during the equation-solving process in case of an attack in collaboration with a miner. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the order splitting strategy, revealing that a substantial portion of profitable sandwich attacks could have been prevented through this approach, leading to significant savings for traders.

Eliminating Sandwich Attacks with the Help of Game Theory

The protocol determines when a transaction is vulnerable to a sandwich attack based on its slippage tolerance and trade size. High slippage tolerances and large trade sizes make the trades susceptible to such attacks. The protocol suggests selecting an appropriate slippage tolerance to strike a balance between the risks of a sandwich attack and the transaction not executing due to price fluctuations. Costs related to failed transactions are factored in, including the price shift between two blocks and the cost of redoing the transaction. The protocol proposes setting the slippage tolerance in such a way that the estimated costs of transaction failure do not surpass the costs of a potential sandwich attack. For large transactions that might face challenges with this balance, the recommendation is to split them into smaller trades to lessen their price impact.
