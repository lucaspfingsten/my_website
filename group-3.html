Trusted Third Party Ordering

The trusted third party ordering approach is gaining rapid adoption in mitigating transaction reordering attacks, particularly in decentralized exchanges (DEXes). Services like flashbots, Eden, and OpenMEV rely on trusted third parties to order transactions, bypassing the Ethereum peer-to-peer network. Some DEXes, like SushiSwap and mistX, have integrated these services directly. In ETH 2.0, a similar private ordering concept is being considered, where transactions are sent directly to block proposers, preventing public mempool exposure. Gnosis Protocol and CowSwap adopt a similar approach by sending transactions directly to solvers, eliminating sandwich attacks within batches. Tesseract and Fairy use trusted hardware and TEE to resist front-running, with Tesseract relying on trusted third parties, while Fairy implements fair ordering using TEE. Trusted third party ordering excels in terms of delay, goodput, jostling, and scope, with no expected increase in transaction fees. However, it may have mediocre cost performance as fees could increase over time. On the downside, decentralization and security are compromised, as ordering depends on a trusted third party's honesty, potentially allowing manipulation and contradicting the blockchain's trustless nature.

Methods

MEV Protect

Multiple providers (Flashbots, Eden, bloXroute, Open MEV, etc.) offer MEV protection services. Users send their transactions directly to the service provider. Thus, the transaction is no longer sent to the public mempool where it can be front run by bots. Instead, these services send bundles directly to miners/validators who have entered into agreements with the respective provider. Some DEXes integrate these services into their UI to make them easily accessible for users. For instance, SushiSwap integrates Eden and PancakeSwap on the Binance Smart Chain integrates TODO.

Professional Market Makers

This approach employs professional market makers in decentralized exchanges to ensure fair transaction orderings. It involves off-chain agreements on exchange rates and on-chain transaction execution, balancing efficiency and security.

BlockSwap

Its a decentralized finance (DeFi) infrastructure platform designed to enhance the utility and liquidity of Ethereum (ETH) across multiple blockchains without the need for traditional bridges or intermediaries. It achieves this through its innovative "multichain composable ETH," which allows users to deploy their ETH in superfluid yield-generating activities across various chains simultaneously, maintaining a direct link to the underlying asset for security and redeemability. By providing a composable infrastructure and a yield singularity, Blockswap enables ETH holders to maximize their asset’s efficiency and yield potential in a secure, trustless environment.

Cowswap

CowSwap is a decentralized exchange aggregator that tackles the problem of MEV by facilitating trading without the traditional order book or automated market maker system. Instead of executing orders immediately, CowSwap matches opposite orders from users within a batch auction system, allowing for direct swaps when a coincidence of wants is found, reducing slippage and eliminating the opportunity for miners to prioritize transactions for profit. If direct matches aren't found, CowSwap uses its underlying Gnosis Protocol to find the best price across several DEXs, ensuring users still get favorable trade conditions while minimizing the impact on the market price and saving on transaction fees.

Gnosis Protocol

Gnosis Protocol is a decentralized exchange protocol designed to enable batch auctions for tokens on the Ethereum blockchain. It employs a mechanism that collects multiple buy and sell orders over a fixed period into a single batch and then uses a price-finding algorithm to determine the optimal trade prices and match orders.          

Tesseract

Tesseract is a cryptographic exchange platform designed to enhance security and performance for cryptocurrency transactions by leveraging a trusted execution environment (TEE). The TEE allows code execution in an isolated, tamper-free environment, ensuring that the operations within cannot be observed or altered.
